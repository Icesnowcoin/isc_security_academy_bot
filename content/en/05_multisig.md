# Multisig Security: Collective Guardianship of ISC Treasury

Standard wallets have a single point of failure: if the private key is lost or stolen, the assets are gone forever. To protect its treasury, ISC utilizes Multi-Signature (Multisig) technology.

### What is Multisig?
A multisig wallet requires multiple independent private keys to authorize a transaction. For instance, ISC treasury management typically uses an "M-of-N" model (such as 3-of-5), meaning at least 3 out of 5 designated key holders must agree before funds can be moved.

### Key Advantages
This setup **prevents single points of failure**, as a hacker cannot steal funds with just one compromised key. It also provides **checks and balances**, ensuring no single individual can misuse the treasury. Furthermore, every multisig action is **transparent and traceable** on the blockchain for community auditing.

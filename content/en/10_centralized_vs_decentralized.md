# Centralized vs. Decentralized: Why ISC Chose the Latter

Understanding the differences between CEX (Centralized) and DEX (Decentralized) is fundamental to protecting your assets.

### Comparison Overview

| Feature | Centralized Exchange (CEX) | Decentralized Exchange (DEX) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Asset Control** | Platform controlled (Not your keys, not your coins) | User controlled (You hold the keys) |
| **Access** | Requires Identity Verification (KYC) | Permissionless and anonymous |
| **Security** | Risks of platform exit, hacks, or frozen assets | Relies on contract security; no central failure point |
| **Transparency** | Black-box operations; potential wash trading | Fully on-chain and transparent records |

### ISC's Philosophy
ISC believes that "Code is Law." By operating in a decentralized environment, ISC eliminates the risk of human interference. Our treasury, liquidity, and governance are entirely transparent, ensuring no centralized entity can freeze or misappropriate community assets.

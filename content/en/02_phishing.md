# Phishing Identification: Spotting Fake Sites and Airdrops

Phishing is the most common method used by attackers to steal crypto assets. They create fake official channels to trick users into revealing private keys or signing malicious approvals.

### Common Phishing Tactics
Attackers often use **fake websites and search ads** that look identical to Uniswap or PancakeSwap, paying for top spots in Google search results. Another common trap is the **fake airdrop**, where mysterious tokens appear in your wallet with descriptions leading to malicious sites. Furthermore, **fake support agents** on Telegram or Discord may proactively message you to ask for your seed phrase or request that you scan a QR code.

### Prevention Measures
Always verify the URL by entering project sites through trusted sources like CoinMarketCap or official Twitter accounts. Be extremely cautious with unknown tokens in your wallet and never interact with them on unfamiliar sites. Most importantly, official staff will **never** ask for your private key or seed phrase.

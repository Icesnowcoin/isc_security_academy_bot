# APR vs. APY: Seeing the Truth Behind Returns

In DeFi projects, you will often see APR and APY. While both represent rates of return, their calculation methods—and your actual earnings—differ significantly.

### APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
APR stands for the annual percentage rate, which is based on **simple interest**. It does not account for the reinvestment of earnings. For example, if you invest $100 at 10% APR, you will have $110 after one year.

### APY (Annual Percentage Yield)
APY stands for the annual percentage yield, which incorporates **compound interest**. It assumes that you reinvest your earnings. If you reinvest your returns daily, the "interest on interest" effect will make your final APY higher than the APR.

### Risk Warning
High returns often come with high risks. While looking at percentages, always investigate the project's sustainability, token emission mechanics, and contract security. ISC focuses on transparent, steady, long-term growth rather than unsustainable "vampire" yields.

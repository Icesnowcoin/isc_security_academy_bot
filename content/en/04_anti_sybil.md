# Anti-Sybil Rules: Ensuring Fair Distribution

A Sybil attack occurs when a single user creates multiple accounts to gain unfair influence or disproportionate rewards in a decentralized network. This undermines the core principle of "one person, one vote."

### Impact on Decentralization
Sybil attacks can lead to the manipulation of governance votes and the draining of airdrop pools intended for a broad community. To combat this, projects use on-chain behavior analysis, social graph verification, and proof-of-personhood tools.

### ISC's Approach to Fairness
ISC implements strict anti-Sybil measures to protect its treasury and ensure that rewards go to genuine contributors. By analyzing transaction patterns and requiring multi-step verification for key roles, we maintain a healthy and equitable ecosystem for all members.
